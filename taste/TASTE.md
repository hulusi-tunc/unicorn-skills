## Strong preferences (seen 4+ times, recent)

- **Restraint with one focal accent.** Near-monochrome/light surfaces where a single element carries the color or energy — a contained gradient glow, one accent hue, a rainbow ring — never allover decoration. [5: portline-dashboard, plus-pricing-card, inline-text-toolbar, bites-diary, profile-card-ab]
- **Oversized numerals as the anchor.** Big editorial numbers ("30%", "87%", "$15", "1232 cal") doing the hierarchy work, with quiet grey labels around them. [4: portline-dashboard, plus-pricing-card, sporty-sports-app, bites-diary]
- **Bespoke data visualization over stock charts.** Custom radial gauges and arc meters designed as objects, not chart-library defaults. [3: portline-dashboard, bites-diary + macro rings]
- **Custom assets that demonstrate, not decorate.** Bento tiles and section art built from real product-UI fragments that tell the feature's story, over stock icons/illustration. [2: ticketapp-bento, portline hero — watching]

## Emerging (2–3 signals — watch)

- **Refinement / before-after thinking** — saving comparison studies where the improvement is consolidation (fold elements together, promote one metric, demote the rest). [2: profile-card-ab, bites-diary]
- **Honest AI affordances** — a color chip or contained gradient marks the AI action, not a sparkle/magic-wand cliché. [2: inline-text-toolbar, portline "AI Strategist"]
- **Light, airy surfaces** with soft rounding and generous whitespace as the default (the Sporty dark theme is the outlier). [several]

## Directions this maps to

Leaning clearly toward **luxury minimal** meets **industrial technical** (from the `no-slop` direction list): restrained light surfaces, oversized editorial numerals, bespoke data objects, one deliberate accent. Not editorial-decorative, not brutalist. When a project's brief is open, this is the team's default gravity — confirm against the client's brand.
